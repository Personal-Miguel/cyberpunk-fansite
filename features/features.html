Features

Introduction

Ray-tracing in video games is enabled by NVIDIA's most recent graphics card series RTX. Ray-tracing is essentially an improvement to a game's light rendering algorithm to be more realistic regarding the angles of the user's perspective. Only a handful of games claim to implement it but even fewer are actively implementing ray-tracing today. Cyberpunk 2077 will be a great system to implement ray-tracing with the active lights of the dystopian technological advanced future.


So What?

Ray-Tracing implements a new workload in Boundary Volume Hierarchy Setup(BVHS), Ray/Triangle Intersection and denoising. BVHS is the data-structure that implements ray-tracing, Ray/Triangle Intersection determines whether a light vector intersects resulting in reflections or shadows and the image produced can be incomplete so the denoising will fill the holes of data. These are new implementations that assist with the points below.

  • Shadows in ray-tracing are rendered by determining whether the vectors from light sources intersect in-game geometry. This operation can vary in workload depending on whether the frame of a game is in a dark location with many light sources. Shadows previously were more general as the technology wasn't able to calculate the many vectors of lights interacting with shapes and their results.
  • The biggest improvements would be reflections as they were again more general and never really clear in image direction or rendered properly. This was improved by increasing rays to reflective areas such as puddles, cars, glass an actual realistic image in for example a car's hood. Previously, reflection was sometimes non-existent.
  • Global illumination is a technique that RTX brings new to a computer graphics which that witch every pixel in the frame has a ray vector positioned from a light source like the sun creating a more accurate representation of shadows and reflections. This technology was never implemented before as anything that could fit inside a desktop could render that many light rays.
  • RTX graphic cards contain special cores called RT cores and Tensor cores. RT cores are specialized to render rays mentioned above greatly decreasing frame rendering time from other graphics cards. Tensor cores are specialized cores to take fewer pixels from a frame but still output a great result through proper sampling greatly decreasing frame time.
